Meet Fatima

Survivor of Female Genital Mutilation

I was 6 years old when it happened to me... I knew what was going to happen, I knew they were going to cut me. I have seen it with my own eyes. It was quite normal for girls. My friends had told me that it was really painful, that it was horrible, so I was terrified. It happened when my mother was away.

Meet Ada

Survivor of Forced Marriage

I was only 15 when my father forced me into marriage. Instead of engaging in a costly legal battle, I decided to flee. Three years later, my family found me and forcibly returned me to my husband. After I refused to have sex with him, my husband, aided by his relatives, tied me up and raped me. The following day, I killed him when he tried to rape me again. I was first sentenced to death before my sentence was turned into five years of prison.

  • What is Forced Marriage?

    15 July 2025

    A forced marriage is where one or both people do not or cannot consent to the marriage and pressure or abuse is used to force them into the marriage. It is also when anything is done to make someone marry before they turn 18, even if there is no pressure or abuse.

  • Data on Forced Marriage

    1 January 2025

    Each year, an estimated 12 million girls aged under 18 marry against their will. This means there are currently more than 650 million women and girls worldwide who married as children.

  • What is FGM

    30 June 2018

    Female genital mutilation (FGM) is a procedure carried out on girls and young women/people with a vulva which involves removing some or all of their external genitals. Find out more about FGM, including how to seek help.

  • Data on FGM

    14 December 2025

    200 million women and girls in the world today have undergone female genital mutilation. Building gender aware data systems is critical to create a level playing field for women and girls.

  • Get help

    12 June 2025

    If you are worried that you might be at risk of FGM, or that someone you know might be, its really important to speak to someone you trust, like a teacher or a doctor. There are also lots of organisations which can help and you will always be taken seriously.

  • What is Gender Equality

    26 May 2018

    Gender equality, also known as sexual equality, gender egalitarianism, or equality of the sexes, is the state of equal ease of access to resources and opportunities regardless of gender, including economic participation and decision-making, and the state of valuing different behaviors, aspirations, and needs equally, also regardless of gender.
